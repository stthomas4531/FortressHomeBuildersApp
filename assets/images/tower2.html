Lodging Near Glacier National Park

The perfect place to stay on your Glacier National Park trip.

As Glacier locals we are always excited to open our doors to those looking to explore Glacier National Park! For those looking for lodging near Glacier National Park with all the convenience and feel of your own home, we have six vacation rentals just minutes from the west entrance of Glacier Park.

Whether you’re a couple on a romantic getaway or off on a family vacation in Montana, we offer a variety of cabins near Glacier National Park. Each vacation rental comes with discounted Glacier Highline Adventure Passes for all guests- a can’t miss Glacier National Park vacation package! The high rope courses and ziplines near Glacier are great activities to add your Glacier Park trip!

GLACIER HIGHLINE’S RED CABIN

This is one of the best cabin rentals near Glacier National Park. Gather around a large custom poured concrete island for your family meals in the eat-in, fully equipped kitchen. You will enjoy the open concept living area, perfect for letting you enjoy each others company while resting from Glacier’s adventures.

Sleeps 6-8

1 bathroom

More Information

The cabin is a 2 bedroom/ 1 bathroom that has been beautifully redone with all high-end amenities and a great rustic cabin feel. The master bedroom has one queen size bed and the second bedroom has 2 queen size beds. The bathroom has a walk-in tile shower and beautiful unique finishes. The cabin also has a full-size washer and dryer.

GLACIER HIGHLINE’S TOWER

Located in the middle story of Glacier Highline Aerial Adventure Park’s Tower- this studio apartment boasts simple MT living. Step outside the doors and set off on an aerial adventure!

Sleeps 4

1 bathroom

More Information

The Tower has a queen size bed and 1 sleeper sofa, with an open concept studio with barn wood slider doors, rustic finishes, beautiful walk-in tile shower. The Tower has a full kitchen and full-size appliances, with a stove top for your cooking needs. Please note, there is no oven.
